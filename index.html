WHAT IS DIVERSION & HOW DOES IT WORK?

A diversion is an opportunity for some offenders to avoid parts of the criminal justice system in exchange for participation in alternative programs that are more effective than traditional responses to crime. Diversions offer a customized response that supports the goal of reducing crime overall, while enjoying immense cost-savings.

People often make bad choices as a result of stress, mental illness, family issues, economic instability, and addiction. Many diversion programs require the offender to admit that they’ve committed a crime, holding them accountable for their actions, while also acknowledging the reasons for criminality.

Diversion works. It works better at the earlier stages of an offender’s criminal justice journey. It only works if it’s free for the offender to participate. Learn more by Exploring Diversions.
